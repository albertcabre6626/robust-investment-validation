# Thesis summary

**Title:** Evaluación robusta de estrategias de inversión: backtesting, sobreajuste y validación estadística

This thesis studies the evaluation of investment strategies as a statistical-inference problem on noisy financial time series. A strategy can show attractive historical performance without genuine predictive power, future robustness or statistical significance, especially when many rules or models are tested on the same data.

The validation protocol combines:

- traditional performance and risk metrics,
- transaction-cost analysis,
- temporal bootstrap,
- studentized White's Reality Check,
- Fama-French five-factor regressions with momentum,
- comparison against benchmarks and passive references.

The methodology is applied to classical technical indicators, language models on synthetic time series, genetic algorithms, Markowitz portfolios and a public/censored evaluation of a proprietary quantitative strategy.
